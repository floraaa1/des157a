Jan 30th, 2025

The article called Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows gives me a strong sense of the significant existence of modal windows. To be effective, they must achieve a balance between visibility and usability, without overpowering the screen or being too subtle. Their size, placement, and interaction design should improve clarity while not disrupting the user's workflow. Additionally, coherence with the overall design language is required. Modals must also contribute to the intended user experience by communicating information that makes sense. Since they interrupt the main interface, careful execution is required to avoid annoyance and make sure about accessibility. This complicates the challenge of developing models, which must take into account both functional and visual aspects in order to offer an engaging yet non-intrusive experience.

Feb 7th, 2025

The article "10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills" captivated me by offering a fresh perspective on how photography can be used to develop visual thinking. Every picture has a backstory that is hidden in addition to the scenario that was photographed. Occasionally, this backstory may differ entirely from how we initially interpreted the picture.

For instance, a picture of a man fixing a broken pipe really caught my attention. Because he was standing in the water, I initially thought he was in distress. Entry was forbidden by the road markers nearby, and a policeman had pulled over, apparently ignoring the man instead of helping him. But after studying the image's context and reading the title, I came to the conclusion that my presumption was completely wrong. This encounter reaffirmed the notion that deciphering a photograph necessitates more than merely visual inspection; it also entails comprehending the scene in which the picture was captured and what is actually occurring within the frame.
