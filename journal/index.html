The article called Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows gives me a strong sense of the significant existence of modal windows. To be effective, they must achieve a balance between visibility and usability, without overpowering the screen or being too subtle. Their size, placement, and interaction design should improve clarity while not disrupting the user's workflow. Additionally, coherence with the overall design language is required. Modals must also contribute to the intended user experience by communicating information that makes sense. Since they interrupt the main interface, careful execution is required to avoid annoyance and make sure about accessibility. This complicates the challenge of developing models, which must take into account both functional and visual aspects in order to offer an engaging yet non-intrusive experience.

Mar 1st, 2025

This week, I read Game Design Principles. Adding exciting features and providing users with a seamless user experience are two critical factors in game design. The article outlined the top seven essential game design principles. One of the most important is setting clear goals and objectives. Clear goals help users understand what they need to accomplish in the game. To achieve these goals, players must complete smaller tasks along the way. If they become confused during gameplay, it could lead them in the wrong direction, break their flow and engagement. Another principle that impressed me is balancing the game. Establishing numerical balance is crucial to ensure the game isn't too easy or too difficult. Well-balanced mechanics ensure that players are appropriately challenged without feeling frustrated, which helps maintain player engagement throughout the game.
